# Week 11 — Challenges

Two open-ended problems. Unlike the exercises, these have **no single right answer** — the skill is judgment under limited data, not recall. Do them after the three exercises.

1. **[Challenge 1 — Forecast with seasonality](challenge-01-forecast-with-seasonality.md)** — you have exactly one year of monthly data and a real-looking August dip. Decide how much of that to bake into a forecast that has to stand up to statistical scrutiny. *(~60 min.)*
2. **[Challenge 2 — Stress-test a forecast's assumptions](challenge-02-stress-test-assumptions.md)** — a finance-approved forecast has a fragile assumption buried in it. Find it, quantify the exposure, and fix it. *(~60 min.)*

## How these are judged

There's no answer key with a single correct number. Instead, each challenge tells you what a *strong* submission looks like. You're being graded on:

- **Correctness of reasoning** — can you justify the method you chose, and its limits?
- **Explicit assumptions** — did you write down every judgment call, in a number, not an adjective?
- **Honesty about uncertainty** — did you say what you don't know, instead of presenting a guess as a fact?

Keep your work in `challenge-01.md` / `challenge-02.md` with your queries/code **and** your written reasoning. The reasoning is the point.
