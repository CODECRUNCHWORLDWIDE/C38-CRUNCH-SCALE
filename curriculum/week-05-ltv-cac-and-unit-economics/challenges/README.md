# Week 5 — Challenges

Two open-ended problems. Unlike the exercises, these have **no single right answer** — the skill is judgment, not recall. Do them after the three exercises.

1. **[Challenge 1 — Model unit economics for two channels](challenge-01-model-two-channel-economics.md)** — build one reusable model that puts `paid_search` and `organic_content` side by side, and decide what it would take for each to become healthy. *(~60 min.)*
2. **[Challenge 2 — Spot an unprofitable growth story](challenge-02-spot-unprofitable-growth.md)** — a VP's growth memo makes a confident case using real numbers from this week's data. Find every place the math is quietly doing something misleading, and show the corrected version. *(~60 min.)*

## How these are judged

There's no answer key with a single correct number for the recommendation itself. Instead, each challenge tells you what a *strong* submission looks like. You're being graded on:

- **Correctness of the underlying math** — every number you cite must be reproducible from the seed data with a query or a script you show.
- **Explicit method choices** — did you say which LTV method, which CAC (blended vs. trailing), and which time horizon you used, and why?
- **Honest handling of uncertainty** — did you flag where the data is thin (small-n retention points, unproven long-term churn) instead of presenting a guess as a fact?
- **A defensible verdict** — your recommendation should follow logically from your own numbers, not from a gut feeling stated first and justified after.

Keep your work in `challenge-01.md` / `challenge-02.md` with your queries/code **and** your written reasoning. The reasoning is the point — a correct number with no justification is an incomplete answer here, same as in a real growth review.
